Improve FCR in Layers for Better Efficiency

Jul 24, 2025

In the world of livestock production, efficiency is key to maximizing profitability. One crucial measure of efficiency is the Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR), which quantifies how effectively animals convert feed into body weight gain or product output. In the case of layer hens, the FCR indicates the amount of feed required to produce a kilogram of eggs. In this blog post, we will delve into the significance of FCR, explain how to measure it in your own flock, and explore effective strategies to improve the FCR in layer hens.

The FCR serves as an essential indicator of feed utilization efficiency in livestock production. A low FCR signifies that hens are converting feed into eggs efficiently, resulting in reduced production costs and increased profitability. On the other hand, a high FCR indicates inefficient production, highlighting the need for improvements.

Measure the FCR in Your Flock:

To easily measure the FCR in your flock, follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Weigh the feed - Record the weight of the feed provided to the hens during the measurement period.

Step 2: Collect egg data - Keep track of egg production over the measurement period by recording the total number of eggs produced by the flock.

Step 3: Weigh the eggs - Weigh a representative sample of eggs produced during the measurement period and calculate the average egg weight. Multiply this average egg weight by the number of eggs produced during the measurement period.

Step 4: Calculate the FCR - Use the formula FCR = Feed intake (kg) / Egg production (kg) to calculate the FCR. For instance, if your hens consumed 30 kg of feed and produced 15 kg of eggs, the FCR would be (30 kg / 15 kg) 2,00.

The average FCR can vary depending on various factors, including genetics, nutrition, management practices, and environmental conditions. However, a commonly accepted range for FCR in laying hens is between 2,5 and 4,0. This means that, on average, it takes 2,5 to 4,0 kilograms of feed to produce one dozen (12) eggs. It is good to know what your FCR is now so you can improve your FCR in the future.

How to Improve the FCR:

Optimizing the FCR is crucial for reducing production costs and maximizing profitability. Following are some effective strategies to improve the FCR in layer hens:

Provide a nutritionally balanced diet: Feeding layer hens a diet that meets their specific nutrient requirements is essential for optimizing the FCR. The diet should contain adequate amounts of energy, amino acids, vitamins, and minerals to support egg production and maintain good health.

Use high-quality feed ingredients: Selecting high-quality feed ingredients that are free from contaminants, mold, and toxins, while having high nutrient digestibility, contributes to an improved FCR in layer hens.

Manage lighting and temperature: Proper lighting and temperature management play a vital role in optimizing the FCR. Providing a minimum of 14 hours of light per day and maintaining a temperature between 15 and 25°C helps to prevent stress and heat-related issues. Strategies, such as shade provision, ventilation, and access to cool drinking water further aid in reducing heat stress and enhancing feed intake and egg production.

Implement good flock management practices: Emphasize good flock management practices, such as regular cleaning and disinfection of housing, disease control measures, and vaccination protocols. These practices improve the overall health and productivity of the hens, leading to better FCR outcomes.
